What They Always Ask

What types of digital products do you design?

I specialize in creating minimal, functional digital products including clothing designs, logos, posters and generally anything creative directed. My focus is on clean aesthetics with powerful functionality.

What is your design process like?

Chaotic. However that speaks with my clients, because of how I keep my process really simple, they can keep up and understand my every step. By doing so, the client knows they have contributed to the creation.

What is your typical timeline for projects?

Timelines vary based on project scope, but I typically work within a week's time, imagining there are not a lot of revisions.

What do you need from me to get started?

To start, I need a clear brief outlining your ideas, target audience, inspirations, and any specific requirements or constraints. Rest is handled by me.

How do you handle revisions and feedback?

I usually include a lot of rounds of revisions in my standard process. I tend to not charge extra for revisions, as I think its just dumb to charge extra for some color changes or scaling issues etc. However, if I judge that the client is being unprofessional by not paying attention to the amount of changes he makes or the adjustment is far too big and will require a lot of time, I am inclined to charge extra. Feedback is valuable in my process, and I encourage clients to express freely, as that helps me and my process to deliver a satisfying outcome.

What is your communication style during projects?

I maintain clear and regular communication with almost-daily updates and check-ins, keeping everything organized and transparent. Though, I'm always available via whatsapp and my social media handles.
